Organic farming is an agricultural system that avoids synthetic fertilizers and pesticides, focusing instead on natural processes. Key practices include:
Soil Health
: Enhancing fertility through composting and crop rotation.
Biodiversity
: Promoting diverse crops and livestock for ecosystem balance.
Natural Pest Management
: Using beneficial insects and natural methods instead of chemicals.
Sustainability
: Emphasizing water conservation and renewable energy.
Animal Welfare
: Ensuring humane treatment and access to outdoor spaces.
Products labeled "organic" must meet specific standards set by regulatory bodies.

Tomatoes are a popular and versatile fruit that belong to the nightshade family, originating from western South America. Known for their juicy texture and rich flavor, they come in various types, including cherry, beefsteak, and heirloom. Nutritionally, tomatoes are low in calories and packed with vitamins C and K, potassium, and antioxidants like lycopene, which is associated with numerous health benefits. In the culinary world, they are used in countless dishes, from fresh salads to hearty sauces and soups, making them a staple ingredient across global cuisines.
